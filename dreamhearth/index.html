DreamHearth

A commons-based bedding system. A place where nothing is asked of you but rest.

What this is

DreamHearth is a modular “nest + shell” bedding system designed to welcome a human into the world, grow with them, and later return to a tighter nest for aging, recovery, illness, or deep fatigue.

No tracking. No optimization. No restraint. Natural-fiber-forward. Built for repair, adaptation, and dignity.

Core geometry

1) The Nest

Modular pillows/bolsters arranged around the body to support curling, side-sleeping, and turning.

2) The Shell (head shelter)

A lowered head-rest with a hooded rim that narrows the visual field and dampens sound—without covering the face.

3) The Weave

Optional arm pockets / side folds for easy in/out and movement-friendly containment.

Lifecycle configurations

  • Arrival (infant): tighter nest, deeper warmth, softer sound/light.
  • Opening (child): modular supports, tactile comfort, movement welcomed.
  • Widening (adult): same geometry with more space; shell becomes a quiet rim.
  • Return (aged / recovering): tighter nest to reduce effort; gentle orientation cues (texture/seams, not color reliance).

Safety & dignity line (non-negotiable)

DreamHearth must always:

  • allow easy exit (fail-open)
  • preserve choice and comfort
  • avoid restraint, locking, compression, forced positioning
  • avoid surveillance, sensors, “compliance” framing

If a “safety feature” violates dignity, it does not belong.

Suggested repo structure

  • /docs/ — principles + notes
  • /patterns/ — written patterns and material notes
  • /configs/ — infant/child/adult/return configurations
  • /research/ — references
  • /license/ — license text(s)

Start simple. Let it grow like a hearth grows: one careful addition at a time.
