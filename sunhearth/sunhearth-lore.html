SunHearth

The canonical entry — a micro-flame instrument of warmth, reflection, and attentive gathering.

Lore Entry · Canonical

The SunHearth is not built to heat rooms or shelters. It is a model world in miniature — a tiny sun cradled in a bowl — used to help students see and feel how warmth moves across shapes, surfaces, and stories.

Every SunHearth rests on three simple principles:

  • The Little Flame — the seed of warmth.
  • The Shaping Bowl — the horizon that gathers and reflects.
  • The Watching Stone — the presence that listens.

When these are placed together with care, they form a small universe: sun, earth, sky. In the Circle of Warmth lesson, students use the SunHearth to explore:

  • how heat can travel by radiation, conduction, and convection,
  • how curved shapes guide light, sound, and warmth,
  • how to measure temperature and reflection,
  • how ancestors survived cold seasons with ingenuity,
  • how even a single flame, tended wisely, can warm many hearts.

The SunHearth belongs to guilds of listening, tending, and noticing. It reminds us that warmth is not only a matter of fuel, but of stewardship, attention, and shared presence.

Symbol Key

When you draw or emboss the SunHearth for students, you can use this simple symbol language:

Flame
A small upward curve or teardrop at the center — the Little Flame.
Bowl
A wide, shallow arc beneath the flame — the Shaping Bowl, like a horizon.
Dome
A soft arc above the flame — the reflecting sky or hood.
Stone
A circle resting at the top — the Watching Stone, a guardian of the flame.
Circle
A faint ring around everything — the gathering of people and stories.
