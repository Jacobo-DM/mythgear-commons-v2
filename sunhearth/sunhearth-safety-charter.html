SunHearth Safety Charter

A reference for teachers. What this is, what it is not, and how to hold it carefully.

Important: The SunHearth is designed as a supervised classroom demonstration for understanding micro-radiant heat, geometry, and safety. It is not a DIY heater design and is not offered as advice for heating homes, shelters, or emergency spaces.

1. General Principles

  • A small flame is used only under continuous adult supervision.
  • The teacher is the only person who lights, moves, or extinguishes the flame and any heated components.
  • The setup is treated as a science apparatus, not as classroom decor or a permanent heater.
  • The room remains ventilated (open door or window) when the flame is active.
  • The demonstration can be paused or cancelled at any time based on student behaviour or teacher judgment.

2. Fuels & Materials

  • Use a single, familiar low-smoke fuel source (for example, one small candle).
  • Do not burn unknown materials, plastics, trash, or improvised fuels.
  • Make sure all surrounding materials are non-flammable and stable (no loose fabric, paper, or plastics near the flame or above it).
  • Treat any metal parts, stones, or bowls as potentially hot after use; allow full cooling time before touching or storing.

3. Student Boundaries

  • Students observe and measure from a safe distance designated by the teacher.
  • Hands may approach the warm zone only when invited and guided.
  • Students never reach in, adjust parts, or blow on the flame, even “as a joke.”
  • Before any flame is lit, the class recites the child-friendly safety mantra (see SunHearth Rhyme & Safety Mantra page).

4. Communication with Families

  • When possible, inform families that the lesson includes a brief, supervised candle-flame demonstration.
  • Emphasize that the purpose is to study how heat moves and how to be responsible with even very small flames.
  • Invite students to share what they learned about safety, not about the device itself.

5. What This Resource Is Not

  • It is not an endorsement of any improvised heating method.
  • It is not a substitute for building-code-compliant heating systems.
  • It is not a recommendation to reproduce the apparatus outside of supervised educational contexts.

MythGear and the SunHearth lesson exist to support curiosity, safety, and stewardship. Please adapt this charter to your local regulations and school policies, and when in doubt, choose the most conservative safety option.
