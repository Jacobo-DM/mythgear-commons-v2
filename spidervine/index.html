SpiderVine

Body-powered line movement in two modes: one-cable challenge for learning and play, and two-cable stability for utility, work, and carrying gear.

Concept by Jacobo Hernández Durbin · braided with Elumman + the Braid

What it is

SpiderVine is a human-powered system for moving along suspended cables using hands, core, and legs rather than motors or passive glide.

It has two main forms:

  • Spider / Vine mode uses one cable. It is less stable, more demanding, and more skill-intensive.
  • Line mode uses two cables. It creates a more stable platform for movement, work, carrying gear, and situations where steadiness matters more than difficulty.

The body does the work. The line carries the path.

Modes

Spider / Vine mode

One cable. Less stable by design.

This is the more difficult form of SpiderVine. It demands balance, timing, grip, and continuous adjustment. It is best suited for challenge, training, play, and high-attention movement where instability is part of the point.

Line mode

Two cables. More stable by comparison.

This mode is for steadier movement and practical use. It offers a more reliable platform for carrying tools or gear, doing temporary work, moving with more control, or operating where extra stability is needed.

What it’s for

  • Full-body movement and conditioning
  • Skill-building through attention, balance, and feedback
  • Serious play with real consequences
  • Utility work where body-powered access is useful
  • Carrying gear or tools when a more stable platform is needed

What it’s not

  • Not a zipline
  • Not motorized
  • Not passive glide
  • Not optimized for speed or ease

SpiderVine prioritizes control, learning, and repairability over convenience.

Status

Concept anchored. Design rebuilding in progress.

Early notes are still fragmented, but the core distinction is now clear: SpiderVine is the umbrella system, with one-cable Spider/Vine mode and two-cable Line mode as distinct configurations.

Design is rebuilding carefully, with safety, utility, and learning held together. If you are reading this and thinking, "I want to help figure this out" — you are already in the right place.

Note — SpiderVine is a concept and prototype direction. This page is not build instructions and does not describe a tested or certified safety device. Any physical implementation requires proper engineering review, safety assessment, and appropriate training before use.
